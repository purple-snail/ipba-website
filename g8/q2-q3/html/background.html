Background

The Inca civilization was developed in ancient Peru between the 1400s until 1533. The empire was located in Cusco (also spelled Cuzco), in their empire there are rankings for men. Men of lower rank could have only one wife; people of ranks higher than the karaka were allowed more. The Sapa Inca could have about 100 wives and 100 children. The political organization of the Inca empire was also based on gender parallelism. The people that are part of the Inca were only allowed to work inside their society. Male and female roles were considered equal in Inca society. The woman had access to land and other necessities. Women's everyday tasks include: taking care of the children, weaving cloth, cooking, planting, harvesting, and carrying water. Men on the other hand are participating in combat, building houses and they are also responsible for sustaining his family. This relationship between the genders may have been complementary. Aztec and Inca usually trade for resources and livestock
