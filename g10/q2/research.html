Synopsis

The study explores the complex relationship between gaming and academic performance in APEC Kalumpang junior high students, identifying both positive and negative effects such as enhanced cognitive skills and social interaction versus academic distraction and potential addiction. The importance of moderation in gaming is emphasized, supported by a survey of 50 students. A previous study suggests gaming can positively impact educational outcomes when balanced with study time. However, concerns are raised about lower motivation, social network addiction, and physical and psychological harm associated with excessive gaming. The study advocates for understanding individual motivations and implementing interventions to support a healthy balance between gaming and academics. It aims to investigate the prevalence of gaming, the correlation between gaming time and academic performance, and the relationship between the types of games played and academic outcomes. The goal is to provide valuable insights into fostering a balanced and supportive educational environment amid the growing popularity of video games. The findings will give students insights to prevent negative effects, help parents understand and support their child's academic journey, and offer valuable information for other researchers in related studies.

Survey Results

Based on the survey that we conducted in the Junior high school, the beliefs and opinions of the students we saw were that 40% of the students said that their academic performance is not affected by playing games while others said their academic performance is affected by playing games because it consumes their time in playing games.

In the survey that we conducted in the Junior high school, the attitude of some individuals may hold the belief that gaming harms academic performance due to potential time distraction, while others may have a more neutral stance, seeing gaming as a form of entertainment that can be managed responsibly. There could also be people who believe that gaming can have positive effects, such as enhancing problem-solving skills and teamwork. Understanding the attitudes toward the effects of gaming on academic performance is essential in creating a holistic view of the student experience at Apec Schools Kalumpang. This presentation aims to explore the perspectives of students.

The survey reveals that 70% of students hold a positive attitude, believing that gaming contributes to the development of problem-solving skills, and 60% view collaborative gaming as a means to enhance teamwork.
